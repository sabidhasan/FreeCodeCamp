Henri Cartier-Bresson

French humanist photographer considered a master of candid photography

Timeline

  • Born in Chanteloup-en-Brie, Seine-et-Marne, France, the oldest of five children.
  • His parents supported him financially so Henri could pursue photography
  • In 1927 Cartier-Bresson entered a private art school and the Lhote Academy. Cartier-Bresson regarded Lhote as his teacher of "photography without a camera."
  • In 1929 Cartier-Bresson met American Harry Crosby, who presented Henri with his first camera. After Crosby's death, Cartier-Bresson went to Africa briefly where he almost died
  • Returning to France, he acquired the iconic Leica camera with 50 mm lens. He photographed in Berlin, Brussels, Warsaw, Prague, Budapest and Madrid.
  • In 1934 Cartier-Bresson met photographers David Szymin and Endré Friedmann (Robert Capa).
  • In World War II, he joined the French Army as a Corporal in the Film and Photo unit, and spent 35 months in prisoner-of-war camps doing forced labor under the Nazis.
  • In early 1947, Cartier-Bresson, with Robert Capa, David Seymour, William Vandivert and George Rodger founded Magnum Photos. Cartier-Bresson would cover India and China. He achieved international recognition for his coverage of Gandhi's funeral in India in 1948 the Chinese Civil War in 1949.
  • Cartier-Bresson retired from photography in the early 1970s, and by 1975 no longer took pictures. He returned to drawing and painting.
  • Cartier-Bresson died in Céreste (Alpes-de-Haute-Provence, France) on August 3, 2004, aged 95. No cause of death was announced.
